# Payment Processor Appeal Strategy: Re-Framing High-Risk Medical AUP

This document outlines the step-by-step strategy for appealing payment gateway onboarding rejections (e.g., Stripe, Razorpay, PayPal) that flag the application as a "clinical diagnostic or health decision-support tool" under high-risk Acceptable Use Policies (AUP).

---

## 1. The Core Re-Framing Strategy
Payment processors reject clinical tools due to liability (medical malpractice risk, FDA regulation criteria). To successfully appeal, you must demonstrate that the platform is **not a diagnostic device** but rather an **administrative simulator and workflow management utility**.

### Pivot the Business Description
* **DO NOT Use Terms**: "AI Diagnostic Tool", "Medical Decision-Support", "Prescription Generator", "AI Doctor", "Treatment Advisor".
* **DO Use Terms**: "Clinical Simulation Training Platform", "Administrative Workflow Automation", "Medical Transcription Assistant", "EHR Interoperability Pipeline", "Billing and Claims Audit Tool".

---

## 2. Text Template for the Official Appeal

When submitting the appeal form or responding to the compliance review team, use the following structured template:

> **Subject: Onboarding Appeal - Organization Compliance & Functional Scope Audit**
>
> Dear Compliance Review Team,
>
> We appreciate the opportunity to clarify our platform's functional scope. We believe there has been a misclassification of our service. 
> 
> Our platform, **[Your Product Name]**, is not a clinical diagnostic device, nor does it provide medical advice or health decision-support to patients. It is a **SaaS Workflow Automation and Simulation Platform** designed for healthcare administrators and clinical students.
>
> Please find our compliance profile below:
>
> 1. **Administrative & Billing Automation Focus**:
>    The primary modules of our software automate back-office clinic tasks, such as telehealth audio transcription (drafting administrative notes), checking claims for formatting compliance to reduce insurance denials, and auditing database logs for HIPAA consent compliance.
>
> 2. **Clinical Training Simulation**:
>    Our patient-facing portal is a *simulated learning environment* for medical students and staff to practice data logging and review mock clinical scenarios. No live diagnostics are performed.
>
> 3. **Strict Non-Diagnostic Guardrails & Disclaimers**:
>    The software does not diagnose conditions or suggest treatments. All AI-generated text is bounded by strict guardrails that mandate a medical disclaimer, explicitly instructing users to consult a qualified clinician. The platform does not replace professional medical judgment.
>
> 4. **Compliance Status**:
>    Our product does not fall under the FDA's Software as a Medical Device (SaMD) regulation, as it serves administrative, transcription, and educational simulation purposes.
>
> We request a manual review of our account so we can demonstrate our administrative dashboard. Please let us know if you require a video walk-through or access to our staging environment.
>
> Sincerely,
>  
> **[Your Name]**  
> **[Your Title / Founder]**  
> **[Your Organization Name]**

## 3. Product & Website Adjustments (Crucial Before Appeal)
Payment compliance auditors will manually inspect your homepage, pricing page, and terms of service. Before submitting the appeal, make sure you execute these adjustments:

### A. Add Site-Wide Disclaimers
Ensure a clear, visible disclaimer is placed in the footer of your public site and login pages:
> *"Disclaimer: [Product Name] is a clinical simulation and administrative workflow utility. It does not provide medical diagnoses, treatment recommendations, or health decision-support. All clinical simulations are for educational and operational training purposes."*

### B. Adjust Pricing Tiers
If your pricing tiers mention clinical tiers like "Clinical Diagnosis Tier" or "Doctor Decision Support Plan", rename them:
* Change "Clinical Diagnosis" to **"Administrative Suite"**.
* Change "Decision Support Plan" to **"Transcription & Workflow Automation"**.

### C. Update Terms of Service (ToS)
Add a dedicated "No Medical Advice" clause in your ToS agreement:
* Explicitly state that the user agrees the software is an administrative assistant and simulated training helper.
* Include a limitation of liability clause explicitly disclaiming responsibility for any health decisions made outside the software's administrative function.
